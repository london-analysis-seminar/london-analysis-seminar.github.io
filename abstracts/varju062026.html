Random polynomials are irreducible

A few years ago Emmanuel Breuillard and I proved that a random polynomial with iid 0-1 coefficients cannot be written as the product of two integer polynomials in a non-trivial way. The result is conditional on a suitable extension of the Riemann Hypothesis. The key ingredient in the proof is the study of certain Markov chains first introduced by Chung, Diaconis and Graham. The method is very flexible and has been extended to study random polynomials in different settings by different authors. These often lead to interesting problems in probability. I will survey these developments in the talk focusing on the probabilistic aspects. The talk will be self-contained. In particular, no prior knowledge of number theory is assumed.
