Arick Shao (Queen Mary, University of London)

Control of parabolic equations with inverse square infinite potential wells

Abstract:

We consider heat operators on a bounded convex domain, with a critically sin- gular potential diverging as the inverse square of the distance to the boundary of the domain. We establish a general boundary controllability result for such operators in all spatial dimensions, in particular providing the first such result in more than one spatial dimension. The key step in the proof is a novel global Carleman estimate that captures both the relevant boundary asymptotics and the appropriate energy for this problem. The estimate is derived by combining two intermediate Carleman inequalities with distinct and carefully constructed weights involving non-smooth powers of the boundary distance.
