Richard Sharp (University of Manchester):

Title: Singular measures and convergence to volume in negative curvature.

Abstract:
A theorem of Bowen (1972) asserts that the periodic orbits of a geodesic flow over a compact surface of constant negative curvature, with periods in an interval [T-a,T+a], become equidistributed with respect to the Liouville measure as T tends to infinity. A sharper version due to Zelditch (1989) gives a rate of convergence and hence equidistribution when the interval shrinks as T increases. In variable negative curvature, the periodic orbits are no longer equidistributed with respect to Liouville measure but, by a result of Parry (1986), an appropriately weighted average is. We will discuss results where the interval is allowed to shrink in this setting. This is joint work with Mark Pollicott.
