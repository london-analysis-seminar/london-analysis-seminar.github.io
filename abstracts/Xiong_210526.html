Pseudodifferential operators with analytic or Gevrey regularity

In this talk, we discuss some classical results on pseudodifferential operators ($\Psi$DOs) that can be improved when their symbols have better regularity than smoothness, i.e. in real analytic or Gevrey classes. We will explore the construction of a parametrix (approximate inverse) of an elliptic operator, and spectral estimates for $\Psi$DOs in this context. We will also introduce some microlocal analysis tools in the analytic or Gevrey scheme, including Sjöstrand’s pseudonorms of formal symbols, $\Psi$DOs on the FBI transform side, and the method of phase space exponential weights.
