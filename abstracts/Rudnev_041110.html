Michael Rudnev (University of Bristol)

Title: A Szemeredi-Trotter/Beck type theorem in prime fields.

Abstract:
Abstract: In the early 1980s J. Beck proved a theorem that for any set P of N points in the Euclidean plane, at least one of the two following claims must hold, for some absolute c: (i) cN of the points lie on the same straight line or (ii) there are cN^2 distinct straight lines determined by pairs of points. A sum- product theorem by Bourgain/Katz/Tao/Konyagin (early 2000s) implied that in a plane over a prime finite field F_p the same type of result must hold, provided that N is by order of magnitude smaller than the cardinality of the plane itself (in the latter case there will be just N lines determined by all pairs of the N points). But if P is a cartesian product AxA, with say |P|< p, then P determines at least |P|^{1+ \epsilon} lines, for some \epsilon >0. However, no quantitative lower bound for \epsilon was known. Presented in this talk is a quantitative version of the theorem (a joint work with H. Helfgott) : using the more recent state-of-the- art sum-product type technique we can prove that \epsilon > 1/267; also discussed are some consequences and connections. It is not clear what the best possible bound here shall be.
