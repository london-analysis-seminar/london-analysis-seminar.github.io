Julie Rowlett (Hausdorff Center for Mathematics, University of Bonn):

Title: From dynamics to spectral theory on complete negatively curved manifolds.

Abstract:
The main focus of this talk is joint work with P. Suarez-Serrato and S. Tapie concerning the dynamical theory of convex cocompact manifolds. These manifolds contain many interesting examples: convex cocompact hyperbolic manifolds, asymptotically hyperbolic manifolds (with variable negative curvature), and the conformally compact manifolds (with variable negative curvature) introduced by Fefferman and Graham in the 1980s to study conformal invariants and more recently studied in mathematical physics based on their role in AdS-CFT correspondence in string theory. We first show that generalized dynamical zeta functions on these manifolds admit a non-vanishing analytic extension (with the exception of one simple pole) to a certain half space. These results imply counting theorems, known as ``prime orbit theorems,'' for the number of closed geodesics. Finally, I will discuss my work in the case of strongly- asymptotically-hyperbolic manifolds, and interactions between the dynamics and spectral theory of these spaces. Physically, such interactions imply relationships between their quantum and classical mechanics.
