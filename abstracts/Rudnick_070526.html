The quantum Rabi model

The quantum Rabi model (QRM) is a fundamental model of light-matter interaction. It describes the interaction between a two-level atom (qubit), coupled to a quantized, single mode harmonic oscillator. It has its origin in the semi-classical model of such interactions due to I. I.Rabi (1936), and its fully quantized version due to Jaynes and Cummings (1963), who introduced a rotating wave approximation" which is exactly solvable and is very useful for small coupling. However, recent advances in experimental techniques reaching the ultra strong coupling regime bring to the fore the need to understand the full QRM, without imposing the rotating wave approximation. Thus the need for a better understanding of the mathematical theory of the QRM. I will describe recent progress on some conjectures that have arisen in the mathematical theory of the Quantum Rabi Model and its generalizations, using tools from the spectral theory of Jacobi matrices, number theory, and algebraic geometry. These will be presented from the viewpoint of a complete newcomer to the subject.
