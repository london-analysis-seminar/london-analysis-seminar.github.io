Mahir Hadžić (University College London)

On gravitational Landau damping

Abstract:

We consider a family of compactly supported inhomogeneous steady states to the gravitational Vlasov-Poisson system with a point mass at the origin. They are parametrised by the polytropic index k>1/2, which measures the regularity of the steady state. We prove the following sharp dichotomy result: if k>1 the linear perturbations Landau damp (i.e. equilibrate in a suitable sense) and if 1/2 < k < 1 they do not. This dichotomy is a new phenomenon and highlights the role of steady state regularity in the discussion of long-time behaviour of (linear) perturbations. The key ingredient in the proof of damping is the exclusion of embedded eigenvalues inside the essential spectrum. The key ingredient in the proof of no damping (1/2 < k < 1) is a suitable version of the Birman-Schwinger principle adapted to the problem at hand. Joint work with G. Rein, M. Schrecker, C. Straub.
