Dave Applebaum (University of Sheffield):

Title: Pseudo-differential operators and Feller-Markov processes on compact groups.

Abstract:
The associated semigroup, generator and resolvent of a Feller- Markov process on $\R^{n}$ may all be represented as pseudo-differential operators, and the converse problem of constructing a process from a given symbol has been intensely studied by Niels Jacob and his group. In recent years Michael Ruzhansky and Ville Turunen have built a global theory of pseudo-differential operators on compact Lie groups using Peter-Weyl theory. In this talk I'll describe how the Ruzhansky-Turunen approach can be applied to associate pseudo- diiferential operators to Feller-Markov processes on compact groups. A particularly interesting class of processes which appear in statistical applications are a class of central Levy processes having laws whose Fourier transforms are induced from the real line by means of the Casimir spectrum. In this case the pseudo-differential operator approach yields an easy proof of Sobolev regularity for the generator.
