London Analysis and Probability Seminar

The London Analysis and Probability seminar is jointly organized and is alternately held by Imperial College London; King's College London; Queen Mary, University of London and University College London. The seminar meets during term time on Thursdays from 3:00pm-5:30pm. Please follow the links below for the details of how to get to the institutions. All are welcome to attend the meetings.

For questions regarding the website, to be added to the London Analysis mailing list or to circulate information, please email london.analysis@gmail.com.

2018 Summer Term Programme:

Thursday, 3 May, 3-5.30pm King's College London, room S5.20
  • 3pm Monica Musso (Bath) Singularity formation in critical parabolic equations abstract (ES)
  • 4.30pm Vitaly Moroz (Swansea) Thomas-Fermi type models of external charge screening in graphene abstract
16-18 May: Conference Analysis aspects of dynamics, Imperial College London

The event aims to bring together experts working on analysis and dynamics, and enhance the interaction between these fields. In particular we will focus on the interface of analytic dynamics and geometric analysis (quasiconformal geometry and quasiregular maps), keeping in mind other aspects as well. For further information, visit the conference webpage.
